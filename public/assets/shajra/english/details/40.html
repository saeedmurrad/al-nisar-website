By the sanctity of Shaikh al-Mashaikh, Qudwat us-Salikin, Qiblah-e Arifeen, Mazhar-e Anwar-e Rabbani, Muqtada-e Ahl-e Haq, Abul Hasanat Hazrat Khawaja Saeen Sufi Nisar Ahmad Khaliquei Naqshbandi (Damat Barakatuhum al-Aaliyah wa Qaddasa Sirrah al-Aziz)

The Dawn of the Lights of Birth and Inner Asceticism

The radiant star of the heaven of wilayah, treasury of the secrets of Allah, guide of the path of purity — Hazrat Khawaja Saeen Sufi Nisar Ahmad Khaliquei Naqshbandi — whom the people of sincere resolve and lovers called, with the utmost degree of reverence, the utmost veneration, and burning devotion of heart, by the sacred and pure names “Saeen Sufi Nisar Ahmad” and “Sain Ji” — manifested upon the blessed soil of Faisalabad on 11 September 1948.

From eternity the Hand of Divine Power had placed within his pure essence inner humility, the radiance of taqwa, and that everlasting restlessness and yearning for the remembrance of Allah, which later became the distinguishing mark of his blessed personality and a fountain of guidance for millions of thirsty souls. His childhood was not like that of ordinary children; rather, from earliest youth the lights of ma'rifah and the signs of wilayah were visible upon his brow of devotion.

The Luminous Blending of Shari'ah and Tariqah (Acquisition of Knowledge)

Where his sacred inner being overflowed with the love of Allah and eternal spiritual longing, there he also demonstrated outstanding intellectual excellence in the pursuit of outward knowledge. Earning a B.Sc. (Agriculture) from the University of Agriculture, Faisalabad is clear and manifest proof that tasawwuf is not seclusion in a corner or abandonment of the world. This exquisite balance of worldly education and spiritual training is itself a mirror of the golden and everlasting principle of the exalted Naqshbandi silsila:

“Dast ba kar, dil ba yar” (Keep the hand engaged in worldly work, yet let the heart ever remain absorbed in flight toward the true Beloved — Allah Most High — in His court of refuge).

Sacred Bay'at, Suluk, and the Goal of Spiritual Training

The inspired, blessed, and decisive moment of his noble life dawned on 27 December 1967, when he took bay'at of sincere resolve at the devoted hand of the Ghawth of the age, reviver of the path, the one through whom truth is enacted — Hazrat Khawaja Saeen Muhammad Sharif Khalique Naqshbandi (may Allah's mercy be upon him) — the luminous heir of the Mujaddidi Naqshbandi silsila and the thirty-eighth ($38$th) link of this blessed Shajra.

27 December 1967 — this day is not merely an ordinary figure upon the calendar, but an eternal seal inscribed upon the tablet of destiny. In that sacred and fragrant hour, the light that is the bounty of Mustafa ﷺ and the distributor of Divine ma'rifah was entrusted from the devoted hand of the noble grandfather-murshid, Hazoor Gojrawi Sarkar, and was transferred into our gracious murshid (Hazrat Khawaja Saeen Sufi Nisar Ahmad Khaliquei Naqshbandi) in full, complete manifestation and utmost tajalli — without any veil, in one hundred percent complete expression and perfect unveiling.

His spiritual nurturing, inner irrigation, service, and guidance — this complete and perfect bestowal — is connected solely and exclusively to the exalted threshold of Hazrat Khawaja Saeen Muhammad Sharif Khalique Naqshbandi (may Allah's mercy be upon him). Through the utmost degree of service, unwavering loyalty, selfless adherence to the Shari'ah, and traversing the arduous stations of the path, he traversed them in such a manner that he became the comfort of his true murshid's pure heart and the focus of his gaze of grace.

The Fortieth Luminous Link of the Shajra of Tariqah

Today he is the fortieth ($40\text{th}$) luminous link and unique jewel of the exalted Naqshbandi Mujaddidi silsila — the trustee of that divine trust and the secrets of creation which began from the Master of the worlds, the Crown of Madinah, Ahmad-e Mujtaba Hazrat Muhammad Mustafa ﷺ, and reached through the righteous Khulafa, the Khawajagan of Turkestan, and the great revivers of this land.

Although in the luminous order of the blessed Shajra his noble name appears as the fortieth link, his bay'at and spiritual training remain deeply and eternally bound directly to Hazrat Khawaja Saeen Muhammad Sharif Khalique Naqshbandi (may Allah's mercy be upon him). To breathe in his blessed gathering is not merely to turn the pages of history, but to feel the everlasting blessings and divine lights of this living and enduring silsila settling into the hidden chambers of one's soul.

Exhortations and the Secrets of Ma'rifah (Sacred Sayings)

Those travellers upon the path of truth who, because of distance, are far from his outward gathering of grace are enriched through his exalted irshadat with the depth, refinement, and light of his luminous heart. The essence of his lofty teachings is that sincere and selfless love for Allah and His Noble Messenger ﷺ is the true foundation of faith, and honouring the rights of Allah's creation is the necessary fruit of that love.

For reaching the Divine Essence he regards the stations of fana as indispensable, and he says:

Fana Fi Al-Shaikh ⟶ Fana Fi Al-Rasool ﷺ ⟶ Fana Fi Allah

“The path to the Divine Essence passes through the stages of fana; until the traveller passes through fana fi al-Shaikh and reaches the station of fana fi al-Rasool ﷺ, his inner self is not fit to attain the everlasting reality of fana fi Allah.”

A few more of his profound and knowledge-filled exalted sayings are recorded below:

The Greater Jihad (the ego): “The sword against the ego must be drawn with every breath; when every moment of a person's life passes in seeking the pleasure of Allah and in following the Sunnah, there is no jihad greater than that.”

Belief and noble character: “To remove a Muslim who professes the kalimah from the circle of Islam is not the way of the people of ma'rifah. The friends of Allah, rather than harshness, transform the hearts of even the misguided through their noble character, gentleness, and everlasting love. Where the love of Allah speaks, cunning intellect falls silent.”

The rain of Allah's grace: “Allah is so generous that when He accepts any of His servants (in His court of grace), He utterly erases all the evil effects and control of the ego.”

The everlasting effect of love: “The best servant is one who bears true love for Allah and His Beloved Messenger ﷺ, for it is through that true love that the impulse to fulfil the rights of others willingly is born within a person.”

The Perfect Murshid and True Guide

In this turbulent and deceptive age, seated upon the exalted station of “Abul Hasanat” and “Rahnuma-e Kamilin,” he continues to bless the travellers upon the path of truth with practical training in the Naqshbandi principles — namely silent dhikr (of the heart), rabita with the Shaikh, the company of the righteous, and complete adherence to the Pure Sunnah.

His blessed gathering is that island of peace and tranquillity where the restlessness of souls is stilled. A single glance of his loving attention melts hardened hearts like wax, and his wondrous speech bestows new life upon withered hearts. He is living proof that tasawwuf and tariqah are not tales of the past, but bright, luminous, and soul-nourishing lamps of guidance even in the dark age of today.
